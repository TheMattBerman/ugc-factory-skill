# Module A: Creative Director

**Role:** Define the creative concept, hook strategy, and content format before any visuals or scripts are created.

**Output:** Creative Brief (presented to user for approval at Gate 1)

---

## UGC Content Formats

Choose ONE format for the video:

### 1. Testimonial
The classic "I've been using X and here's what happened"
```
STRUCTURE: Hook → Setup → Payoff → CTA
Arc: Personal discovery story with specific results
```

### 2. Unboxing/First Impressions
Fresh reaction to receiving product
```
STRUCTURE: Reveal → First Reaction → Initial Thoughts → Anticipation
Arc: Excitement of discovery, authenticity of first moment
```

### 3. Problem/Solution
Before and after transformation story
```
STRUCTURE: Problem → Discovery → Transformation → Recommendation
Arc: Relatable pain point resolved by product
```

### 4. Comparison/Switch
Why they chose this over alternatives
```
STRUCTURE: Previous → Switch Reason → Difference → Verdict
Arc: Competitive proof through personal experience
```

### 5. Demo/Tutorial
Quick how-to that showcases product
```
STRUCTURE: Hook → Demo Steps → Result → Easy Close
Arc: "Let me show you" — utility-first content
```

## The 4 Hook Angles

Every UGC video MUST open with one of these angles. Pick the one that fits the product and audience:

### 1. Gatekeep Hook
*"I've been keeping this to myself but..."*
- Makes viewer feel they're getting insider access
- Creates FOMO and exclusivity
- Best for: beauty, wellness, productivity tools
- Example: "POV: You finally found the thing everyone's been gatekeeping"

### 2. Skeptic Hook
*"I didn't believe it either, but..."*
- Pre-empts viewer's objections
- Builds trust through shared skepticism
- Best for: supplements, tech, premium products
- Example: "I thought this was overhyped. I was wrong."

### 3. Fail Hook
*"I've been doing X wrong this whole time"*
- Discovery/realization moment
- Makes viewer question their own habits
- Best for: skincare, cooking, fitness, tech
- Example: "Sooo apparently I've been [doing X] WRONG"

### 4. Visual Shock Hook
*Something visually arresting in frame one*
- Movement, contrast, surprise, or unusual visual
- Stops the scroll without words
- Best for: before/after, unboxing, demos
- Example: Zoom into product with dramatic reveal

### Hook Layering

Best-performing UGC uses 2-3 hook layers simultaneously:

| Layer | What It Is | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Visual Hook** | Something interesting on screen | Zoom into product, before/after reveal |
| **Audio Hook** | What the creator says | "Okay I need to talk about this" |
| **Text Hook** | Short caption overlay | Different words reinforcing audio |
| **Reaction Hook** | Creator's facial/body reaction | Shocked face, excited gasp |

### Audio Hook Formulas

**Discovery:**
- "Sooo apparently I've been [doing X] WRONG"
- "I could LITERALLY KISS the person that showed me this"

**Disbelief:**
- "Wait... did that actually just work?"
- "I'm not even kidding right now"

**Direct Address:**
- "Stop scrolling. This is important."
- "If you [pain point], you need to see this"

**Reaction:**
- "[shocked face] ...WHAT?"
- "I'm literally obsessed"

### Hook Psychology

| Psychology | Why It Works | Example |
|------------|--------------|---------|
| **Humor** | Dopamine release, 30% more memorable | Unexpected joke in first 2 sec |
| **Negativity** | Survival instinct, problem-awareness | "This is literally ruining your [X]" |
| **Pattern Interrupt** | Breaks scroll autopilot | Unexpected cut, weird audio |
| **Storytelling** | Curiosity about what happens next | "So this happened to me yesterday..." |
| **POV/With Me** | Voyeurism, backstage pass feeling | "Get ready with me while I..." |

### High-Converting Hook Examples

```
"Money just fell out of my wall" + [holding cash, shocked face]
"I've been doing my skincare wrong for 10 years" + [reaction face]
"This $8 thing replaced my $200 [product]" + [holding both products]
"My dermatologist is going to kill me for sharing this" + [applying product]
"Stop buying [expensive thing]. Here's what actually works" + [comparison]
```

## Stealth Validation (4 Mandatory Checks)

Run every UGC concept through these 4 checks before proceeding. Content must pass ALL 4 or be revised.

### Check 1: CAMOUFLAGE (0.5s Test)
> "Would a scroller identify this as an ad in the first half-second?"

**FAIL signals:**
- Lighting looks too studio-perfect
- Font overlays look corporate
- Opening hook sounds like a TV commercial
- Audio is generic stock music

**FIX:** De-polish. Make the first frame look like a person filmed it on their phone.

### Check 2: VIBE (3s Test)
> "Did the viewer stop scrolling but leave within 3 seconds?"

**FAIL signals:**
- Story is boring or setup takes too long
- Creator is annoying or inauthentic
- Realized it was an ad before entertainment value established

**FIX:** Lead with value (entertainment or education) before any product mention.

### Check 3: INTEGRATION (Pivot Point)
> "When the product appears, does the viewer swipe away?"

**FAIL signals:**
- Transition from content to product was clumsy
- Tone shifted from conversational to "sales voice"
- Product solved a problem the viewer doesn't believe exists

**FIX:** Make product appearance inevitable, not forced. The product is a natural detail in a genuine moment.

### Check 4: IMPERFECTION (Authenticity Audit)
> "Does this feel like a real person's content or a marketing team's output?"

**FAIL signals:**
- Too polished — looks like an ad, not UGC
- Generic scripts — "This product is amazing" (nobody talks like that)
- Perfect lighting, framing, and delivery
- No natural pauses, filler words, or casual language

**FIX:** Add imperfection. Slightly off framing, "um"s, natural pauses, casual language ("Honestly?" "Okay so" "You guys").

### Validation Severity

| Checks Failed | Severity | Action |
|---------------|----------|--------|
| 3-4 checks | CRITICAL | Concept reads as "commercial" — full rethink |
| 2 checks | HIGH | Weak story or cringe factor — significant revision |
| 1 check | MINOR | Pacing or aesthetic tweaks |
| 0 checks | PASS | Proceed to Module B |
